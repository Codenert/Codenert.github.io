Core Reflection

Wow, what a journey has been, facing and overcome different challenges, working both individually and in pairs, sharing ideas and thoughts, and putting myself out there to help others so I would say that it's been a great journey. Throughout this journey of learning, I found out the main three things I learned about myself as a result of core learning, both my strength and limitation. I have both a growth and fixed mindset, I also realized that I have low emotional intelligence and lastly I enjoy challenges and have good problem-solving skills. Realizing these limitations I have for myself as a human being I am grateful that core content taught me skills and techniques on how to improve on these areas. I've learned that our brain is capable of building and strengthen new paths how this can have a major benefit for a growth mindset. I've learned mindfulness, conscious listening, and journaling and how they are so important to train my emotional intelligence. The framework and process I learned have improved my problem-solving skills to the next level.

Role of values, empathy and self-awareness

Values, empathy, and self-awareness are very important in learning and programming. To understand the roles of values, empathy and self-awareness I have to look back on how I applied it during the foundation. As I mentioned in my sprint 1, I value time and hard work because we cannot achieve things if we don't put much time and hard work and this applies in both learning and programming. During the foundation, I was able to put myself out there to help those who needed help. Instead of being quiet and focus more on me, instead, I put myself in others situation, willingly to help them, embraced them when they managed to solve their problems and that is empathy and self-awareness in my practice.

What surprises me

What surprises me about the core learning is how effective the skills and techniques are in both human skills and problem-solving skills. However, from learning and applying it during the foundation has helped to change me to better understand myself, how to work and communicate effectively with people, how to use my time wisely, be able to give myself sometimes besides spending my time on the screen.

What was challenging

The most challenging aspect of the core learning for me was applying emotional intelligence training. I remembered when I have to do the conscious listening challenge, at first I was nervous and negative thoughts came into my mind saying that I cannot do it. But then I overcame it when I remembered about a growth mindset so I did it anyway and I was proud of myself because it became easy later on when I did the pair programming challenge.

Why focusing more on a core learning

At Dev Academy, we spend most of our time on core learning even though we know it's a web developer school. There is a really good reason behind that because they want us to prepare ourselves for our future work in the tech industry. In the tech industry, employers are looking for people with good human skills rather than someone so knowledgeable in coding. Being a software developer is to be able to deliver a message and share ideas and thoughts with other people.

So from my personal experience, I think it is not a waste of time studying the core learning because human skills is very important in the workplace. Think about it, a company is a place where people from different cultures come and work together. Being able to understand what you will bring will have a major impact on people in the workplace. These techniques and skills that we learned are so important not just for the workplace but for us to apply them during our learning journey at Dev Academy and possibly be able to apply them in our long-term careers.
