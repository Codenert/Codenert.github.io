Neuroplasticity

Neuroplasticity exposes how the brain works and is designed to do, which is the ability of the brain to adapt to changes through a year of its lifetime. There are two types of neuroplasticity, functional and structural neuroplasticity. Functional neuroplasticity is the ability for the brain to replace connections internally this can help people to recover from different kinds of brain sicknesses such as traumatic brain injury, stroke, or other brain damage. Structural neuroplasticity is the ability to strengthen and create new connections based on experiences. This can be beneficial as well for people's abilities to learn new things or improve their skills in certain areas.

By understanding the principle of neuroplastic can have a major impact on us throughout our learning journey in life. We should know that we are not born to be good at something or born with skills, but instead knowing that it is our job to fill and expand our brains by building and strengthed new connections.

To learn new things, we just need to do it, it is our brain's job to build that connection in our brain. To be good at something, we just need to practice it. However, all of these will be meaningless unless we take good care of our brain properly that is by giving our brain time to rest such as having a good sleep every day. Exercising also has a benefit to our brain's health.

Growth Mindset

Growing Mindset is a way of thinking that an individual thinks positively toward challenges and also sees the challenges as opportunities to grow. Why this is relevant? Well, we all know that in our journey in life we always have to face challenges either in our personal life or at work. An understanding of a growth mindset can help us to overcome those challenges which helps us to reach our goal.

Growing up until now I never knew about Growth and Fixed Mindset. As a young kid growing up I always do well in primary school, I always get a price for becoming 2nd sometimes 1st place in my class, and my friends always think that I am smart. I could not figure out why I'm smart the only thing that I have in my mind is that I got it from my mother thus I was born with it. However, in secondary school math was one of the subjects that I hated the most because I always failed my math test. As a result, I did not attend my math class. There was one time, before the final exam I realized that I have to pass my maths exam so that I can get into high school. The school policy in Kiribati is that for a student to get into high school they need to pass every subject. Anyway, I told myself that I have to attend my math class and believe that I can do it and it was surprising that after putting in my effort and believing that I can do it and when the exam came, I passed my math exam, and from then on maths became my favorite subject.

The concept of Growth and Fixed mindset is something that I came across as a young kid during my time at secondary school and still fascinates me because I don't like math and now math became one of my favorite subjects. Based on my experienced and knowing that there are two types of mindset fixed and growth, I believe that I can grow and have the potential by putting in hard work, see challenges as opportunities and knowing that my abilities, talents and intelligence can be developed.
